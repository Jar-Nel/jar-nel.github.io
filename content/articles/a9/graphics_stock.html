Stocking Stuffer

Pictures bring visual interest to a website. You want images on your site to grab the attention of the reader but images and photographs are protected by copyright. You shouldn't use pictures you don't have permission or a license to use.

So what are the options? You can either use your own images (as I have done on this article), you can get permission from the creator of an image, or you can make use of stock photography.

Stock photography consists of professional photographs that are bought and sold on a royalty-free basis and can be used (and reused) for commercial purposes. Often, a license is purchased from the artist or publisher to use their work. Fortunatly there are a number of places where stock photography can be found and licensed for free.

Sites for free stock photography:

  • Pexels

    Pexels bills itself as "the best free stock photos shared by talented photographers" and they have quite a collection. Photos are tagged by subject and category.

    Pexels Website

  • Unsplash

    Unsplash has a decent collection of high quality photography, tagged and searchable.

    Unsplash Website

  • Burst

    Burst contains a smaller collection of free pictures, but what it has is generally high quality.

    Burst Website

  • Wikimedia Commons

    Free imagery that is used on Wikipedia. The site takes contributions like Wikipedia, so there's a lot to choose from. It's probably the best site for technical imagery.

    Wikimedia Commons Website


Differrent sites have different content, so you may need to search multiple sites before you find what you're looking for.
